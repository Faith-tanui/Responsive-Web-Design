A tribute to Nelson Mandela

Here is a timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

1918: Born Rolihlahla Mandela on the 18th of July in South Africa

1918- 1934: Early childhood. Mandela attended a local methodist school when he was about seven. Baptised a methodist, he was given the English name "Nelson" by his teacher.

1934-1940:Mandela began his secondary education at Claekebury and after completing his Junior certificate joines Fort Beaufort college in 1937. In 1939, he began his BA degree at the University of Fort Hare where he befriended Oliver Tambo and K.D. Matanzima. Although he had friends who had connections to the ANC(Africa National Congress), Mandela distanced himself from the movement. In his second year, he was suspended from the university for being involved in a boycott led by the SRC(Student Representative Council).
